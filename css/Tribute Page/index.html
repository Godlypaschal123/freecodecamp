Chinua Achebe: Voice of African Literature

Chinua Achebe was a Nigerian novelist, poet, and critic best known for his first novel, Things Fall Apart (1958), which is considered a classic of modern African literature. His work explored themes of colonialism, identity, and cultural conflict.

Throughout his career, Achebe received numerous accolades, including the Man Booker International Prize. He was a strong advocate for African voices in global literature and inspired generations of writers across the continent.
